Course structure

The weekly loop is simple. Learn, attempt, test, correct.

Olympiad progress is made through cycles. A student learns a concept, attacks difficult problems, sits a test, then receives correction. The next week begins from the mistakes.

01

Lesson 1: concept depth

Theory, examples, and problem patterns taught from Olympiad standards downward.

02

Homework: hard practice

Students submit written solutions or code, not only final answers.

03

Lesson 2: correction

Tutor review focuses on reasoning gaps, presentation, and traps.

04

Weekly test

Timed pressure turns training into measurable progress.
